Introduction to the Topic

An endangered species is a species that is very likely to become extinct in the near future, either worldwide or in a particular political jurisdiction. Endangered species may be at risk due to factors such as habitat loss, poaching and invasive species. Currently there are more than 32,000 species thar are threatened with extinction, that is still 27% of all assessed species. The visualisations below will look where the species are being endangered, how and how many are being endagered and what are we doing to improve the situation.

Role of International Organisations

IUCN Red list is an international organisation which monitors species and classify them according to their numbers. The stacked bar chart on the left shows how many percentages of assessed species are classified in the 7 IUCN categories. The bar chart shows data for four kingdoms of living species.

International Whaling Commission (IWC) was established in 1943 to "provide for the proper conservation of whale stocks and thus make possible the orderly development of the whaling industry". After its' establishement whale populations in some regions of the world have rebounded and are increasing. The world map shows membership status of all countries in the World in IWC.
